MUSI 2525 Intro to Audio Tech I

This course covers the fundamentals of audio technology. Student learning outcomes include deep understanding and practical familiarity with concepts such as signals and systems, electro-acoustics, sound effects, synthesis, and music protocols.

Instructor: Jocelyn Kavanagh

Term: Fall

Course Overview

Students will gain critical understanding of, and hands-on experience with the fundamentals of audio technology. Student learning outcome will include deep understanding and practical familiarity with concepts such as signals and systems, electro-acoustics, sound effects, synthesis, and music protocols. Upon completion of the course, students will demonstrate:

  • critical understanding of audio signals and systems
  • knowledge and critical understanding of the fundamentals of analog audio technology, electro-acoustics, audio effects, and music protocols,
  • the ability to understand the signal flow in typical equipment for audio processing, and
  • the ability to use this knowledge to create a technological artifact for audio processing.

We discuss topics such as the sinusoidal nature of sound, amplitude and phase control, wavetables, sampling, synthesis, modulation, delays, and filters. A trained music technology scholar is able to consider these techniques through the lens of multiple disciplines. At the end of this course, you will be able to:

  • Use mathematics and physics to model the expected behavior of these audio technology principles.
  • Solve practical problems related to each of these concepts in an audio engineering context.
  • Identify the use of these techniques in music recordings and live audio software output.
  • Implement these techniques using a real-time programming language (Max).
  • Choose appropriately from this palette of tools to create a music technology artifact.

Textbooks and References

  • “The Computer Music Tutorial” by Curtis Roads
  • “The Theory and Technique of Electronic Music” by Miller Puckette

Schedule

Week Date Topic Materials
1 Max Intro; Sinusoids

Starting off with Max/MSP. Defining frequency, amplitude, and phase
  • First Day Lecture
  • Sinusoids Lecture
2 Controlling Amplitude and Frequency

Understanding amplitude measures and envelopes. Understanding pitch vs frequency and control signal in Max.
  • Amplitude Lecture
  • Frequency Lecture
3 Time in Max

Sequencing and controlling time. Lists and metro in Max. Discussion on panning
  • Panning and Sequencing Lecture
4 Additive Synthesis and Digital Audio

Classical waveforms and additive synthesis. Digital audio concepts including sampling, quantization, and PWM
  • Additive Synthesis Lecture
  • Digital Audio and Sampling Lecture
5 Wavetable Synthesis and Section 1 Review

Wavetable synthesis implementation and reasoning, relationship between sampling rate, length of buffer, and output frequency
  • Wavetable Synthesis Lecture
  • Exam Review
6 Sampling Synthesis and Synthesizers in Max

Sampling Synthesis vs Wavetable Synthesis. Process for building instruments in Max
  • Sampling Synthesis Lecture
7 Sound Synthesis Techniques

Step back and discuss/introduce music synthesis techniques. Introduce modulation synthesis including FM, AM, ring, and LFOs.
  • Music Synthesis and Faust Lecture
  • Modulation Synthesis Lecture
8 Modulation Synthesis

Deeper dive into frequency modulation and amplitude modulation synthesis.
  • Modulation Synthesis Lecture
  • Modulation Math
9 Synth Building and Phasors

Discuss and implement polyphony to conclude synth building techniques in Max. Introduce sinusoids as phasors with discussion on complex sinusoids.
  • Polyphony Lecture
  • Sinusoids as Phasors Lecture
